Biography

Pa. Emmanuel Adeyeye was born into the family of Baba James Ipinnimo & Mama Felicia Aina Ipinnimo on the 21st day of July, 1940 at Otunjelu Quarters, Ijelu-Ekiti. He grew up in strength and stature and was fortunate to begin his primary education in St Paul’s Anglican School, Ijelu-Ekiti in 1953.

He transferred his studentship to Methodist School Itapa-Ekiti in 1957 (a distance of about 7 kilometers to Ijelu which was covered by foot on daily basis), where he graduated from in 1959. He proceeded to Anglican Secondary Modern School Itaji-Ekiti in 1960 and because of the leadership characteristics seen in him, he was made the head boy of the school in 1962 & finally graduated in 1963.

His quest for further studies took him to St John/Mary’s Teachers Training College Owo in 1965 where he was awarded with Grade II Teachers Certificate in 1970.

Baba worked a few years before he finally got admission into the Great Ife (Now Obafemi Awolowo University, Ile-Ife) in 1975. After sleepless nights of serious hard work, God granted him grace and bagged a degree in Social Sciences in 1978.
